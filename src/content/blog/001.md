Whether people are excited about it or skeptical of it, AI is everywhere now. You hear about it in conversations regarding ChatGPT, self-driving cars, and even toothbrushes. Due to the term being used everywhere, its meaning has started to feel vague.

Artificial Intelligence, at its core, is the field of building computer systems that can perform tasks that usually require human-like intelligence. AI does not mean the computer can think like a person. It usually means the system learns patterns from data and uses those patterns to make predictions or decisions.

Let's say someone wanted to create a filter to detect spam emails. Rather than writing every possible rule for detecting spam emails, an AI system could be trained on a large collection of example emails and learn what spam usually looks like.

While AI is powerful, it is far from perfect. It can misunderstand things, hallucinate, repeat bias from its training data, and worst of all, it can be extremely confident while being extremely wrong.

Learning AI basics is important for everyone as it will impact every field. Tech is one of the clearest examples. Areas like software, cybersecurity, cloud, robotics, and even hardware have all been transformed in just a couple years.

Whether you like it or not, AI is here, and that means learning how to use it is extremely important.
